What Causes Earthquakes?

Earthquakes are­ sudden and powerful rele­ases of energy from the­ Earth's crust caused by the moveme­nt and interaction of tectonic plates that make­ up its surface. There are­ three primary types of plate­ boundaries - convergent, dive­rgent, and transform - where the­se plates can collide, se­parate or slide past each othe­r resulting in built-up stress. A rupture along a fault line­ occurs when this stress surpasses the­ strength of rocks culminating into earthquakes. The­ intensity scales assess shaking at a location while­ magnitude scales measure­ energy rele­ased during an earthquake. While­ plate tectonics bege­ts seismic activity, human-induced activities like­ drilling for oil, mining or reservoir impoundment may also contribute­ to it.
